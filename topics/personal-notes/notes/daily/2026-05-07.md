- **Consumer-side websocket close — feasibility evaluated, deferred** (Immix streamfailed thread; companion to [[2026-05-06_immix-streamfailed-worker-lifespan]] and the outbound [[2026-05-06_immix-streamfinished-inquiry]]). Question: can we close the AutoPatrol websocket from the puller side before Immix's DeviceWorker hard-lifespan timer tears it down, and would that flip the UI label off `streamfailed`? Mechanically yes — drafted a 3-line change in `actuate-libraries:feat/autopatrol-consumer-close-experiment` that adds `local_close_bound = min(duration, self.connection_duration)` to `consume_stream`, switches the inner loop bound, and adds a `while/else` log to distinguish race-win vs race-loss in NR. Existing 16 puller tests passed. Branch reverted, nothing committed/pushed. **The label-flip is uncertain** because Immix's worker explicitly logs `Worker has a hard life-span` (sounds unconditional) and we have no observed signal in their logs that they'd categorize a consumer-initiated close differently. **Race timing matters:** their 10s timer starts at *encoder-init* (T+2s), our 10s would start at *first-frame-received* (T+2s+δ where δ is network/buffer latency, ~100-500ms). At margin=0 we lose by δ — change becomes a no-op, experiment proves nothing. To win the race we need ≥1-2s margin, which costs frame coverage: at native 3.8 fps × downsampled to ~1-2 fps, a 2s margin loses ~2-4 inference frames per patrol = 15-20% of the inference window. AutoPatrol product/ops sign-off needed before sacrificing coverage for a cosmetic label fix. Deferred pending Immix's response on the StreamFinished inquiry — if they confirm server-side derivation or expose a write endpoint, no connector change needed.
